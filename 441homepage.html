lesson plan:

1.constructing a lesson plan

1.set your objective for the lesson
2.Include the standards that you are covering in your objective
3.provide an overview of the lesson activities
4.Map out your activities and timeline for the class
5.Include the formative or summative assessments you will use
6.list the instructional materias you need for the class

2.Adjusting Your Lesson Plans Efficiently

1.Script out what you are going to say if you are nervous
2.Allow for some wiggle room in your timeline
3.Tailor your lessons to suit your students needs
4.Use a variety of different instructional styles to keep things fresh
5.Design your lessons to account for different learning styles
6.Over-plan in case you run out of material
7.Make it easy enough for a substitute to perform your lesson
8.Keep a few spare lessons in your back pocket if things go wrong

3.Presenting the Lesson

1.Warm your students up with a bell ringer activity
2.Set expectations and present the key information
3.Oversee some guided practice for rote skills
4.Check the student work and assess their progress
5.Do a freer practice to let students try things on their own
6.Leave time for questions
7.Conclude the lesson with some upbeat praise and final notes
